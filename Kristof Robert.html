01 — About

The whole person, not the symptom.

I believe the body and mind aren't separate clients — they're the same one, asking for attention in two different languages.

I started in sports massage working with athletes who kept coming back with the same injuries. The tissue would heal, but the patterns didn't. That's what led me to NLP — because fascia holds stories, and the nervous system learns them faster than we think.

Today I work with two tools: my hands and a structured conversation. Some clients come for the table. Some for the chair. Most, eventually, try both. The goal isn't relaxation or mindset hacks — it's coherence between what your body is doing and what your life is asking of it.

02 — The two modalities

So — what exactly is it?

Two words you'll see a lot around here. Here's the plain-language version of what they mean and what they actually do for you.

For the body

Sports Massage

Targeted, deep-tissue bodywork built around how muscles, fascia, and nerves actually move — not a spa rubdown.

It blends deep tissue, trigger-point therapy, myofascial release and active stretching. Pressure is firm and purposeful — we work where tension, scar tissue or compensation patterns are restricting you.

The aim isn't just to feel good for an hour. It's to release chronic holding, restore range of motion, speed up recovery between training sessions, and prevent the small issues that turn into injuries.

Good forAthletes, runners, climbers, desk-bound bodies with stiff necks, anyone recovering from a strain.
A session feelsIntense in places, specific, then noticeably looser afterward — often for days.
For the mind

NLP EN / PNL FR

Same thing, two languages: Neuro-Linguistic Programming in English, Programmation Neuro-Linguistique en français.

NLP is a practical toolkit for how language and thought patterns shape your behaviour — the internal dialogue, the images you run on repeat, the beliefs you picked up somewhere and never questioned.

In a session we map the pattern that's keeping you stuck, then use structured techniques — reframing, anchoring, timeline work — to reprogram it. It's a coaching approach, not therapy: we work forward, on outcomes.

Good forStuck goals, performance anxiety, limiting beliefs, stress loops, decisions you can't seem to make.
A session feelsLike a focused conversation with exercises — structured, surprisingly concrete, and rarely what you expected.
